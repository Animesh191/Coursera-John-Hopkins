Study of impact of Natural Disasters on Health and Economy

opts_chunk$set(echo = TRUE, include=TRUE)

Synopsis

NOAA data of natural calamities was studied. Impact was studied from economic point of view as well as public health angle. Data since 2001 was analysed. Under economic impact, property damage and crop damaged were considered. Under health impact, fatalities were given a higher weightage than injuries.

It was found that storms and floods cause most economic damage. Public health though imperilled at rough seas, is most damaged in excessive heat conditions.

Data Processing

This clean, filtered and transformed data has been stored in the local directory under filename “Recent.data”. The code to obtain the clean data is appended at the end of this report.

The Data Transformations -

The trailing script will download the requisit data - if already not done so.

I have used data downloaded on 24th July 2014 for this report.

The URL is [“https://d396qusza40orc.cloudfront.net/repdata%2Fdata%2FStormData.csv.bz2”]

Since the original data is large with 37 columns, we pickup only 8 columns of interest to us viz

  • BGN_DATE - the date of the event
  • EVTYPE - the type of event (there are 985 factors)
  • FATALITIES - Number of deaths
  • INJURIES - Number of non-fatal injured people
  • PROPDMG - Amount of property damage
  • PROPDMGEXP - unit of Property Damage
  • CROPDMG - Amount of crop damage
  • CROPDMGEXP - unit of crop damage amount

There are 902297 observations in the raw data To take only relevant information, we drop data prior to 2001. We also skip lines that are summaries.

Applying the units to the property and crop damage amounts, we get uniform dollar amounts.

Results

With respect to population health Excessive heat is the single largest contributor to damages across the United States.

Hurricanes, Storms and floods cost most economic consequences across the nation.

Floods are also most likely occurence of natural calamities.

Appendix - Preprocessing Source Code

library(data.table) #Fetch and Read Relevant columns of Data if(!file.exists(“./Storm.bz2”)){ download.file(url=“https://d396qusza40orc.cloudfront.net/repdata%2Fdata%2FStormData.csv.bz2”, destfile=“./Storm.bz2”, method=“curl”) }

colClass = c("NULL", "character", rep("NULL", 5), "character", rep("NULL", 14), "numeric", "numeric", "numeric", "character", "numeric", "character", rep("NULL", 9) )


Storm<-read.table("./Storm.bz2", header=T, sep=",", colClasses=colClass)


Storm$BGN_DATE <- as.Date(Storm$BGN_DATE, format="%M/%d/%Y %H:%M:%S" )

#Filter Data #Filter out older than 2001 - when the event types were standardized #Filter Summary lines

Recent<-(Storm[Storm$BGN_DATE > as.Date("2001/01/01") & ! (grepl("Summary", Storm$EVTYPE)),])

#Get the property damage figures in Dollars Recent[Recent$PROPDMGEXP == “K”,“PROPDMG”] = Recent[Recent$PROPDMGEXP == “K”, “PROPDMG”] * 1000

Recent[Recent$PROPDMGEXP == "M","PROPDMG"] = Recent[Recent$PROPDMGEXP == "M", "PROPDMG"] * 1000000


Recent[Recent$PROPDMGEXP == "B","PROPDMG"] = Recent[Recent$PROPDMGEXP == "B", "PROPDMG"] * 1000000000

#get the crop damage figures in dollars Recent[Recent$CROPDMGEXP == “K”,“CROPDMG”] = Recent[Recent$CROPDMGEXP == “K”, “CROPDMG”] * 1000

Recent[Recent$CROPDMGEXP == "M","CROPDMG"] = Recent[Recent$CROPDMGEXP == "M", "CROPDMG"] * 1000000


Recent[Recent$CROPDMGEXP == "B","CROPDMG"] = Recent[Recent$CROPDMGEXP == "B", "CROPDMG"] * 1000000000

#Store filtered data write.table(Recent, file = “./Recent.data”)
